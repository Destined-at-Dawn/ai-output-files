# English Cover Letter Templates

> Most tech internships don't require a cover letter. When they do, keep it short and specific.

---

## Template 1: Internship Application

```
Dear [Hiring Manager / Recruiting Team],

I am [Name], a [year] student at [University] studying [Major]. I am writing to
express my interest in the [Position Title] at [Company].

[1-2 sentences about why this company/role interests you — be specific about their
product, technology, or mission.]

My relevant experience includes:
- [Project 1]: [What you built + quantified result]
- [Project 2]: [What you built + quantified result]

I am confident that my experience in [specific skill] aligns well with the
requirements of this role. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how I can
contribute to [specific team/project].

Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Name]
[Phone] | [Email] | [LinkedIn/GitHub]
```

## Template 2: Cold Outreach (No Posted Opening)

```
Dear [Name],

I came across [Company]'s work on [specific product/technology] and was impressed
by [specific detail]. I am a [year] student at [University] studying [Major],
with hands-on experience in [relevant skill].

[1 sentence about your most relevant project/achievement.]

I would love to learn more about opportunities at [Company] — whether internships,
research collaborations, or simply a conversation about [technology area].

I have attached my resume for your reference. Thank you for your time.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

---

## Tips for Non-Native English Speakers

- Keep sentences short and clear
- Use active voice: "I designed..." not "The system was designed by me..."
- Have a native speaker review if possible
- Don't use translation tools for the whole letter — write naturally
- Focus on technical substance over flowery language
